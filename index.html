3 ways volunteering supports mental health

May is Mental Health Awareness Month. Volunteering is a proven way to boost mental health, not just for this month, but for the long run. Here are 3 ways volunteering benefits you – and your community!
VOLUNTEER TODAY

01. Bring fun and fulfillment to your daily routine.

Whether you volunteer for a one hour consultation call or a long-term project, volunteering is a feel-good activity. It’s been shown to decrease stress and anxiety, and improve your overall quality of life.
Fulfill your daily routine

02. Give back value to your community

Giving back provides you with a sense of purpose and community as you lend your skills and expertise to the cause area you’re passionate about. There’s nothing quite like the sense of accomplishment after helping someone.
Give back to your community

03. Build connections with others

Volunteering builds relationships that go beyond a call or project. Meet new people and learn new skills–many of our volunteers go on to become repeat volunteers, board members, or friends of the organization they initially volunteer for.
Build connections

The power of volunteering

“Once the connection is made, it’s so fulfilling to see the whole process and see the project have a real impact on people’s lives. It makes you feel like you’ve done something right, and we all need a little bit of that in our lives.”
